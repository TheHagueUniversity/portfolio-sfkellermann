Coding vs. Art:
Techno Vibes

Background

Objective

Use processing to create an interactive design including shapes, colors, user input, conditionals, functions, loops, and arrays.

Role

This was a solo project, although I asked for lots of feedback!

Duration

1 day

Link

Programming Bootcamp Report

Project Setup

I started learning processing bootcamp style, in a room filled with 80 other students. I was fascinated. I knew that learning to code would be useful (ahem, for example in building this website), but I did not know it could be used for creative purposes.

When I got this assignment, I decided immediately that I wanted to make a leaf with pieces falling off of it. As you may have gathered from the photos on this page, that idea didn't work out so well. It turned out to be a bit too advanced for my 5th day of learning processing!

Design Decisions & Iterations

I was disapointed that my leaf idea didn't work out, so I started sort of doodling on my processing canvas. I had been playing with different color interactions all week, so I decided to throw a bunch of ellipses onto my canvas in different colors. I also decided to make a version in greyscale, which turned out pretty cool so I made that into an interactive feature (press the 'g' key to change to greyscale).

Then I remembered that we had seen an example of a 3D image made in processing, so I decided to add that to the mix. I noticed that when the ellipses collided on the canvas, they formed these really cool fractal patterns.

As I started playing more and watching my design come to life, I realised that I was tapping my feet to the beat of the techno I had been listening to all day (80 students in one room get a bit loud). I knew what my design was missing: music! After some trial and error, I succeeded in linking music to my project.
