Usability Testing:
Rijksmuseum Amsterdam

Background

Objective

Perform user trialling on the Rijksstudio section of the Rijksmuseum website.

Role

I worked with a team of 4 others on this project. I performed 3 user trials, compiled my data, and collaborated with the team to write the final report.

Duration

4 weeks

Link

Final report on user trialling

Research

We began this project by doing a deep dive into the Rijkssmuseum section of the Rijksstudio webpage. We wanted to look around and find out what possibilites were offered.

After looking around, we realised that we were already encountering usability issues. The next step was coming up with our research questions.

I worked really hard to create a strong setup to the research, including a detailed observation form for keeping track of user actions. In the end, the strong setup was key to help us thoroughly analyse our results.

Conclusions

We found that sadly, while the Rijksstudio offers extremely cool features, like zoom without loss of image quality, these are not at all clear to users. There is even a section where you can download a work of art and use it to make something of your own. Again though, users were unable to locate or understand this feature.

I learned from this project that research can actually be pretty fun. My main takeaway from this project is that the key to good research is a strong setup, and if working in a team, communication and collaboration will either make or break the project. In this case, happily I'm proud of the results!
