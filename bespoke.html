Bespoke Design:
Hallogram

Background

Objective

To create a bespoke intangible gift giving experience, including a card design tool and digital unwrapping experience, with Hallmark as the client.

Project Role

Worked in a duo to perform research and design a prototype for the digital unwrapping of the gift.

Duration

7 weeks

Link

Prototype in figma

Research

To answer our research questions, my partner and I used creative research techniques to interview, observe, and explore our assigned users.

I learned that my user is ambitious, has a strong sense of aesthetics, and cares deeply for his family. Based on these insights, I made him a Dutch-style ‘Surprise’.

Watching my user unwrap his gift helped me further refine my ideas for creating a user persona and then my design vision.

Finally, my teammate and I combined our work and developed a unified design vision and persona.

Design Decisions & Iterations

After completing our unified design vision, my teammate and I each took responsibility for one element of the final project. I took on the Digital Unwrapping.

I started by crafting some HMW questions, and then (bearing in mind that the first idea is never the best idea) I sketched and sketched and sketched some more! I love words, so my sketches tend to be on the wordy side.

Once we decided on our concept (to make a DIY hologram!), I used my love of words to come up with the name of our design: Hallmark + Hologram = Hallo-Gram!
